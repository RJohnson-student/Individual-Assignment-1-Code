AI Businesses

AI-Based Adapted Learning Systems

As there are different kinds of learners out there, a good learning system is one that can adapt to
the unique needs to the users instead of having to deal with multiple lesson plans.(M, 2026)
An AI-Learning system can provide customized experiences by how the students learn. Examples of this is that it can
offer images and graphs for visual learners, while providing mnemories for textual learners. With all this advantages, they can also
raise some ethical issues. Some of which includes:

  • Data Privacy and Security

    • These platforms continuously track highly detailed student data, including academic performance,
    interaction histories, and behavioral patterns. This creates risks for unauthorized access,
    data breaches, and a lack of adequate consent protocols

  • Algorithmic Bias

    • If an AI is trained on narrow or historically biased datasets, it may produce discriminatory results against
    minority populations or learners with disabilities. These systems risk reinforcing existing educational inequities rather than reducing them.

  • Risked Student and Teacher Relationships

    • While AI can automate some tasks, it cannot replace the human interaction and emotional support provided by teachers.
    There are concerns that over-reliance on AI could erode the teacher-student relationship and the social skills students
    develop in the classroom.(Client Challenge, n.d.)

But there are solutions to these concerns, which is, firstly, have a diverse dataset/database. Train AI models to gather a wider variety of demographic
data, socioeconomic backgrounds and inclusive data that represents the current world. Secondly, execute data minimization and encryption. Only collect
bare minimum of data required for personal learning and to use end-to-end encryption to protect sensitive students' records. And lastly, provide a "Human-in-the-Loop"
system. Have AI be a teacher's assistant than to replace the teacher entirely, to bring human words to the students than from a machine with no emotion.
Some examples of AI learning systems would be StudyFetch, a study website with an AI tutor to help you revise your material; Hello Talk, which is a
social networking language learning app that uses AI to help you with grammer and tone before you send them to native speakers on the app. Others include Recall, Traverse,
Studley, etc.

Twelve Pillars Summary

This summary will go over the chapters 4-7 of the book. Chapter 4 talks about pillar 4, which is to "Achieve your Goals". The main theme here is that a life without
concrete goals is a life lived by chance. Charlie advises Michael that the act of writing down goals transitions them from fleeting thoughts into realities.
Goal setting forces you to stretch, adapt, and build the character required to achieve those benchmarks. The material reward is secondary to the person you become in the process.
Chapter 5 is about the Proper Use of Time, something I myself don't know how to use properly sometimes. This chapter focuses on the differences between being busy and being productive.
Charlie teaches Michael that time is our most precious asset, and we must become the active designers of our days rather than letting our days run us. When not knowing how to manage your time
wisely, there are two pains that comes with it, discipline and regret.

These I've known personally well, as when I don't have proper time management, my discipline withers and sometimes I work last minute
and put unnecessary stress on myself, causing myself to get sick and regret my choice. But with time, I've started to learn to make better use of my time and to be more discipline.
Chapter 6 says to surround yourself with the best people. Charlie warns Michael about the profound, often subconscious power of association. The people around you inevitably shape your habits,
your thoughts, and your destiny. Sometimes you would have to sit down and evaluate your friend circle and think to yourself, "Who am I around?", "Are my finds having my best interests at heart?"
, "What effect are they having on me?", etc. So, when you want to have a good relationship with the people in your life, take out the people who brings down your self-esteem or
mental health and keep the ones who make you feel like you belong and are willing to help you, no matter what your situation may be. Finally, chapter 7 speaks about being a
Life Long Learner. The focus here shifts to continuous self-education. Charlie highlights that institutional education is only a baseline. "Formal education will make you a living.
Self-education will make you a fortune." From this chapter, the main point is that true learning begins after school ends. Cultivating a habit of deep reading, studying successful people,
and remaining curious acts as the catalyst for wealth, physical health, and spiritual alignment.
